Intro

I have over 10 years of experience working in web development mostly as a full stack developer. Currently, I work as Technical lead at Encora Inc, where I lead a cross-functional team, working on 4th ranked US based real estate product named Movoto Real Estate, which forms a bridge between seller, agent & potential buyers.

My mantra in life is to learn something new everyday. I'm deeply passionate about creating good quality, high performing, distributed & scalable product.

In the past, I have worked with Quikr building real estate portal QuikrHomes up from scratch. Prior to that, I was working at Flipkart as part of their checkout team. I also have experience in working on a digital publishing platform product.

When I am not working, you can find me exploring new places, I love driving too. When at home you can find me looking over my plants in my balcony garden.

I also like meeting new people over a cup of coffee. Feel free to reach out at manash.khamai@outlook.com or DM on Twitter @MaK_mf.

When I joined Mindfire, I was the only one selected from my college. May be Chinmay(The CEO) saw something in me. I was happy knowing that I would be joining a company soon after graduating, but again I was sad over the fact that most of my friends, branchmates who are hired in bunch by MNC's & will be having fun there together, when I'll be alone here amongst unfamilar faces.

But now when I look back, I am glad I made the decision to join Mindfire. Whatever I am today, well most of it, is because of Mindfire, there's no denying that. Work in there was really challenging. As a fresher you would be put in some experience guy's shoes. You have to take care of the entire software development cycle, starting from designing an application to the final launch & you have to start delivering, right away. The hours I had put in during that time, the late nighters, the endless learning happened due to switching to a new thing every few months, the exposure I got out of it, was priceless.

Thank you Mindfire for shaping me up, making me realize my potential. And special thanks to the bunch of talented mentors, leads I have had in there too, guiding me in time of need.

At Impelsys, I was part of the core product development team. Impelsys is in to digital publishing & we were only 2/3 devs working on the core product, which helped me learn how enterprise solutions are built. How you can scale things up, how you can sell the same product with customization to diff. market vendors like TMH, Sesame Street etc.

In my time there, I integrated the entire payment gatway, added the partial payment based on subscription or as we call EMI feature, for which I got the employee of the quarter award. I was part of the team which built library model, where you can have concurrent check-in/check-out of books & also added a lot of feature to the core product.

When I came to Bengaluru, working in one of the top notch product company was an ambition I have had since long. Working in the checkout team of Flipkart, I gained exposure on how you build scalable solution & how you solve customer facing problems.

I remember the first Big Billion day happened around the time I was there & it was chaotic. For 3 days I was in office for most part of it. It was a good learning curve.

I still remember the day when made my mind to leave Flipkart. I thought the work would be something else but it was not be & I was not happy with what I was doing there. It was one of the toughest decision I have to make. Yet I am glad I did that.

Quikr was moving their base from Mumbai to Bangalore & most people in the office were new joinees. I worked in the Real Estate vertical of Quikr. My main responsibility as a SSE was first & foremost to code, code well, adhering to the proper coding standard as well as helping the juniors/others in the team in need, doing code reviews, tasks related to subversions, maintaining different environments etc.

The technology stack mainly being used were angular js, vanilla js & PHP. The work was great, as we started building the Real Estate vertical from scratch. The work atmosphere was amazing, as I got to work with some very taltented people, till it all lasted.

I still reember the first launch of real estate vertical of Quikr happened @ 2:30 in the night of a Thursday. For the whole week, we were putting up really really late nighters & finally we launched the product in time, as to what stakeholders were asking for.

I had started here as a NodeJs developer, builing microservices, before transitioning on to leading the team & later expanding to take control of website & the ecosystem built around it.

Technically I feel I have grown a lot during last 3-4 years as I have got exposure to work on various things, to name a few: NodeJs, Express, Angular, MongoDB, MySql, MSSql, ELK stack, VueJS, Firebase, Docker, NGINX etc.

As the complete stack is on AWS, so I have got chance to work on a lot of AWS services, such as EC2, ELB, S3, Cloudfront, Athena, Lex, Lambda, Cloudwatch etc.

Also as the product I work on require it to be highly performant, we also use tooling such as lighthouse, pagespeed insight, WPT etc to take stock of how our pages are performing & tuning so as to make it highly performing, better usability, SEO compliant etc. The work is challenging, there's lot of learning on the way too, which keeps me invested.
